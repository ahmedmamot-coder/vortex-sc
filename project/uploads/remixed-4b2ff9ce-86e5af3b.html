PHASE 1BUILD NOW · MOSTLY BUILT

Coach core: the club's operational engine

Everything coaches and admin need to run the club day to day. This is the part already standing in the demo. The job here is to perfect it and make it rock solid before anything is added on top.

A

Squad & access

Admin and coach roles

PIN login per user. Admin runs the club, each coach manages only their own squad.

Squad structure

Six training groups from beginner squad to elite, each with its own roster and coach.

B

Planning & poolside

Training plans & set builder

Structured sets with repeats, distance, stroke, interval, rest, and labels, plus goal and equipment tagging.

Pace clock

Live poolside countdown that runs the session set by set, with rep tracking and a next-set preview.

C

Attendance & testing

Attendance

One-tap session attendance per squad, with trends and history per swimmer.

Test sets

Log repeated training times per swimmer and event, with the best time tracked. Separate from meet results.

D

Meets & performance

Meet management

Entries with seed times, heats, and lanes, a status lifecycle, and a printable results program.

Hy-Tek import

Import the meet file and pull every swimmer's times in. Auto-creates new swimmers and updates PBs.

File import, no live API

Swimmer profiles & PBs

Each swimmer's page leads with best times. This profile is the spine of the whole product.

PBs by course: LCM / SCM / SCY

Per-swimmer analysis

Time progression, splits, and season trends for each swimmer.

W

WhatsApp sharing, kept as a Phase 1 bridge

Coaches push plans and schedules to families over WhatsApp, manually. This stays in Phase 1 so families get value before the portal exists. Limited to plans and schedules only, never a minor's results or body data, and treated as a stopgap that Phase 2 replaces.

PHASE 1BBUILD NEXT · ON A STABLE CORE

Coach edge: the differentiators

The features no generic club app has. These are the heavier, riskier builds, so they come after the core is perfected, on stable ground and with real coach feedback behind them. If this work slips, the club still has a working product.

1

Coaching intelligence

AI lesson-plan review

The AI checks each coach's plan against the club guidebook and suggests edits, keeping coaching consistent to one standard.

Configurable: each club loads its own guidebook

Dryland & fitness

Assign and log gym and dryland work alongside the pool program.

2

Body & nutrition

InBody scan import

Body composition tracked over time on the profile, read next to the performance trend.

File import from LookinBodyMinors: consent-gated

Nutrition

Per-swimmer nutrition guidance and logging, tied to body composition over time.

PHASE 2AFTER THE CLUB CORE IS TRUSTED

Family access: open the club to parents and swimmers

Once coaches are reliably entering plans, attendance, results, and times, the data is ready to expose. Families come second by dependency, not preference. There is nothing to show a parent until the coach side is solid.

F

One family account

Single login for parent and swimmer

One access covers both. The swimmer's training, PBs, and results live inside the family account, with multi-child switching.

Family portal

PBs, results, training plans, attendance, upcoming meets, and test history, all on one view families return to.

N

Notifications

Automated alerts

Class reminders, schedule changes, new results, and promotions sent to parents and swimmers. This replaces the manual WhatsApp bridge from Phase 1.

Consent & visibility

Controls over who sees a minor's body data and coach notes, built in from the start.

Parked for Phase 2: for the older competitive squads, a shared family login means parent and swimmer see exactly the same thing. A separate swimmer view for 15-plus athletes is a detail to settle when we reach this phase.

PHASE 3COMMERCIAL · AFTER THE CLUB IS COMPLETE

White-label: sell the finished club product

Once the full club product (Phases 1, 1B, and 2) works for us, the same product sells to other clubs. Most clubs run on the same operational core, so the build is reused and only a thin layer changes per client. We sell the engine. The methodology stays theirs to bring, and ours stays ours.

Configurable per client

What changes

  • Brand: logo and colours set to the client's identity
  • Guidebook: each club loads its own handbook for the AI review
  • Governing-body standards and meet structure
  • Course units, language including Arabic right to left
  • Currency and local payment rails
Fixed & shared

What stays the same

  • The core engine and feature set from Phases 1 to 2
  • One codebase, so updates ship to every client at once
  • The data model: profiles, meets, PBs, attendance
  • The AI review feature itself, minus the content
Stays ours

What we keep

  • Our coaching guidebook and methodology stay private
  • Clients get the engine, not our content
  • Our own swimmer and club data is never shared
▲

Localisation is the part that looks small and is not

Governing-body time standards, language direction, and payment rails are real engineering, not a colour swap. Budget for them as a localisation layer, and decide which standards and which markets we support first, so selling to other clubs does not quietly get filed under minor modifications.

PHASE 4LATER

Academy: the learn-to-swim side

Same platform, a different audience: beginners, younger ages, and the parents paying for lessons. Built after the club is complete and the white-label motion is proven. The scope below is an outline, to be detailed in its own pass.

Registration, trials & admissions

Online sign-up, trial booking, and trial-to-enrolment conversion.

Learn-to-swim level ladder

Water confidence through stroke development, with promotion logic.

Parent dashboard & progress

Weekly skill updates and transparency for lesson families.

Skill assessment & badges

Stroke checklists, readiness scoring, and digital certificates.

Scheduling, make-ups & waitlists

Class timetables, make-up requests, and capacity management.

High-volume billing

Packages, auto-pay, and statements for large numbers of lesson families.

Outline only. Full academy scope, including how it shares the swimmer profile and progression model with the club side, is defined in a later pass once the club is complete.

What the four of us have already agreed, and what still needs a call before the developer moves.

Decided

Already agreed

✓Build order: coach core, then coach edge, then family access, then white-label, then academy.
✓Perfect the whole club (Phases 1, 1B, 2) before white-label and academy.
✓Split Phase 1 into a core and a 1B, so the basics ship and stabilise before the heavy features.
✓Meet results and InBody come in by file import, not a live API.
✓Best times stored by course, LCM and SCM and SCY kept separate.
✓One family login covers parent and swimmer together.
✓AI review ships to white-label clients, each with its own guidebook. Ours stays private.
✓WhatsApp stays a Phase 1 bridge, plans and schedules only, replaced by Phase 2 notifications.
