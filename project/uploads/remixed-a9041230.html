ENGINEERING SPEC · FOR THE DEVELOPER

Club Platform: build specification

What the platform is, the pieces it is made of, and the order to build them. This is the technical companion to the founders' scope. It is a mobile app for iOS and Android on a shared backend. It is stack-agnostic on the backend, the existing prototype informs the Phase 1 UI, and the zone rules from the club guidebook are written here as the spec to encode, not as a reference to chase down.

01

System overview & tenancy

A mobile app for running a competitive swimming club, on iOS and Android: coaches plan and run training, meets and times are tracked, families get visibility, and the same product is later sold to other clubs. One codebase, many clubs, each club configured by data.

Shape of the system

  • A cross-platform mobile app for iOS and Android, talking to a shared backend API.
  • Single multi-tenant backend. A club is a Tenant; all data is scoped to a tenant.
  • Our first and only live tenant is Vortex Swimming Club. White-label tenants come in Phase 3.
  • The existing prototype informs the Phase 1 UI. Native or cross-platform is the developer's call.
  • Backend needs an API, a relational store, auth with roles, file parsing, an LLM call path, and background jobs for push notifications and retest prompts.

Two ideas the build rests on

  • The zone engine is the core domain. Sets are tagged by zone; the engine holds each zone's rules; the AI checks plans against them. See section 04.
  • Config over code. What differs between clubs (brand, method, squads, zone rules, locale, currency) is per-tenant data. The engine and features are shared. See section 09.

The zone engine

This is the heart of the product. Encode it as data in ZoneRuleset, not as logic baked into code, so a white-label client can load their own. The values below are our six-zone (American) ruleset from the club guidebook.

Heart rate & T-pace

E-1 ~50+ below max · E-2 30-40 below

SP-2 at / near max · SP-3 n/a

tPace/100 = 1000m trial ÷ 10
or = 400m trial × 2.5 ÷ 10

Rest-drift rules VALIDATION

  • E-3 rest too long, the set drops to E-2. Too short, it climbs to SP-1. Hold 15-20" per 200m.
  • E-2 too much rest loses the stimulus. Too little pushes it into E-3 or SP-1.
  • SP-3 rest must be longer than the work (1:6 to 1:10). Less, and it becomes SP-2.
  • SP-2 uses short, incomplete rest so lactate stays high. SP-3 uses full rest so it stays low.
Volume caps belong in the ruleset too. SP-3 examples by squad: PRE technique dives only, ADV 6-8×25m, JNR 8×25m, SNR 8-10×25m, VTX 8-10×25m or 6×50m, LGD 6-8×50m. The reference books from the founders extend these per zone, so model volumeCap per zone and squad rather than hard-coding one number.
05

AI lesson-plan review

Two layers. A deterministic rules engine does the checks against ZoneRuleset. An LLM handles language: explaining flags and drafting plans. Keep the verdicts in the rules engine, not the model.

Rules engine DETERMINISTIC

For each set in a plan, check against the squad and the ruleset:

  • Squad-zone permission, for example PRE or ADV in E-3, SP-1, SP-2 fails.
  • Work-to-rest matches the zone, with the rest-drift rules from section 04.
  • HR target sits in the zone's band for the swimmer's age.
  • Volume within the squad and zone cap.
  • E-2 and E-3 paces are derived from a current T-pace, not free text.

LLM layer LANGUAGE

  • Guide mode: walk the coach through structuring the session section by section, warmup, pre-set, main set, cooldown, suggesting sets that fit each part for the squad and goal.
  • Edit mode: explain each flag in plain coaching language and propose a fix.
  • Draft mode: generate a full session from zone targets, squad, and the swimmer T-paces.
  • The LLM never overrides a rules verdict. Rules decide pass or fail, the model writes the words.

Plan flow

coach builds with AI → review returns to the coach → plan goes live

Plan status is simply draft then final. There is no head-coach approval step and no submission deadline, since there is no approval system to enforce one. The AI output returns to the coach who owns the plan.

06

Integrations

What connects to the outside, and how. Two are file imports we control, one is a live sync, the rest are services.

ODO

Odoo Appointments TWO-WAY SYNC

Attendance and Odoo appointments stay aligned in both directions. Marking attendance in the app updates the Odoo booking, and an Odoo booking creates the session and its expected attendees here. Via Odoo's API, store odooApptRef on attendance.

HYT

Hy-Tek FILE IMPORT

Import the meet results file, parse it into entries and results, auto-create unknown swimmers, and update personal bests by course. No live API. Parsing is the work.

IBD

InBody scans IMAGE / PDF UPLOAD

Scans are uploaded as an image or PDF and stored on the profile next to the performance trend. The test cadence is also provided as a PDF. Body data is visible to coaches, the swimmer, and the parent.

WA

WhatsApp PHASE 1 BRIDGE

Manual sharing of plans and schedules only, never a minor's results or body data. A stopgap removed once Phase 2 notifications land.

LLM

LLM provider SERVICE

Powers the AI review language and draft mode. Provider, hosting, and what data may be sent for a minor are open (section 10).

NTF

Notifications PHASE 2

Push, email, and SMS for reminders, schedule changes, results, and promotions. Replaces the WhatsApp bridge.

PAY

Payments PHASE 3+ / ACADEMY

Local rails, Qatar first. Heaviest in the academy with high-volume lesson billing.

▲

Hy-Tek import on a phone needs thought

Hy-Tek meet files live on the meet computer. On mobile the app takes them through the device share sheet or file picker. Decide whether meet-day import needs a tablet layout or a small admin companion, since it is awkward on a phone. InBody is fine on a phone, since it is just a photo or PDF.

08

Data sensitivity & access control

The platform holds minors' personal and body data. Treat it as the most sensitive thing in the system.

Sensitive fields

  • Identity and travel: date of birth, passport number, QID.
  • Body composition from InBody scans.
  • Coach notes attached to a minor.

Controls

  • Field-level access, not just per-record. Coaches and the family see the profile and body data, but identity documents stay restricted.
  • Body data from InBody is visible to coaches, the swimmer, and the parent. Date of birth, passport, and QID are the most restricted.
  • Encryption at rest for identity and body data, plus an access audit log.
  • The WhatsApp bridge never carries any of this.
▲

Decide the data-protection baseline early

We start in Qatar, so confirm the local requirements for holding minors' identity and health data before M1 ships profiles. It is far cheaper to build the access model in now than to retrofit it.

09

White-label & multi-tenant config

The line between what a client configures and what we keep shared. Get this boundary right in M1, even though selling is M4, because retrofitting tenancy later is painful.

Per-tenant config

  • Brand: logo and colours
  • Method: 5-zone or 6-zone
  • Squads: count and names
  • ZoneRuleset: their zone rules
  • Governing-body standard
  • Locale, Arabic RTL
  • Currency and payment rail

Shared & fixed

  • One codebase
  • The zone engine itself
  • The AI rules framework
  • The data model
  • Core features M1-M3

Stays ours

  • Our ZoneRuleset content, the six-zone guidebook
  • Our reference-book rules and example sets
  • Our club's own data
The engine is the product, the rules are the content. Clients get the engine that tags sets by zone and validates plans. They bring their own rules. Our guidebook never ships with a client tenant.
▲

Mobile white-label means an app-store decision

Selling to other clubs as a mobile app forces a choice: one app that themes itself per tenant after login, or a separately branded app per client in each store. The second gives each club its own identity and listing but multiplies release and review work. Decide before Phase 3.

11

Non-functional notes

Cross-cutting things easy to miss until late.

Course units

Keep LCM, SCM, and SCY separate everywhere a time appears. Never merge bests across courses.

Timezones

Sessions, meets, retests, and notifications all need a clear, consistent timezone.

Internationalisation

Build for translation and full Arabic right-to-left from the start, needed for white-label.

Offline & poolside

The pace clock and attendance run on a wet pool deck with patchy signal. Build offline-first, cache locally, and sync when back online.

Security

Role and field-level access, encryption for sensitive data, consent records, and an audit log.

Scale

Squad and athlete numbers grow, and white-label adds tenants. Nothing should assume one club.

Push notifications

Use native push for the Phase 2 alerts. Handle permissions and deep links into the right screen.

Devices

Phones for parents and swimmers. Coaches may want a tablet poolside, so design layouts for both.

Build the engine once, drive it with data. The zone ruleset, the roles, and the tenancy boundary are the three things worth getting right before the rest is layered on.
